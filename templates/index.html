Max Pain (OI)
The strike price where option writers pay out the least (option buyers lose the most).
Total Pain(P) = Call Pain + Put Pain
Call Pain = Σ max(P - K, 0) × Call OI(K)   |   Put Pain = Σ max(K - P, 0) × Put OI(K)
The strike with the minimum total pain is the max pain point.
Based on yesterday's closing OI data. Reflects accumulated positions over days/weeks.
P = hypothetical expiry price  |  K = strike price of each option contract  |  OI = Open Interest (number of outstanding contracts at that strike)
Max Pain (Volume)
Same formula as OI Max Pain, but replaces OI with today's real-time trading volume at each strike.
Call Pain = Σ max(P - K, 0) × Call Volume(K)   |   Put Pain = Σ max(K - P, 0) × Put Volume(K)
Useful on expiration day (0DTE) to see where today's active traders are concentrated.
Limitation: Volume includes both opening and closing trades — cannot distinguish new positions from closed ones.
Volume Distribution Chart (Call / Put)
The middle chart shows per-strike trading volume (bars) and open interest (dashed lines) for calls and puts separately.
Green bars (up) = Call volume at that strike   |   Red bars (down) = Put volume at that strike
Green dashed line = Call OI   |   Red dashed line = Put OI
How to read: Spikes in volume show where traders are most active today. Compare with OI lines to see if today's activity aligns with existing positions or diverges.
Volume is real-time (~15 min delay from CBOE). OI is from yesterday's close (updated daily by OCC).
How to Combine OI Max Pain & Volume Max Pain
Scenario Signal
OI MP ≈ Volume MP (same strike or very close) Strong magnet. Today's trading reinforces existing positions. High confidence the price is attracted to this area by expiration.
Volume MP shifts away from OI MP Repositioning in progress. New money is flowing to different strikes. On 0DTE, Volume MP may be more relevant than OI MP since the new activity drives near-term hedging.
Price is between OI MP and Volume MP Tug-of-war. Existing positions pull one way, today's flow pulls another. Watch which side has higher total notional value.
Volume is very low (no meaningful Volume MP) OI MP dominates. Without significant new trading, yesterday's OI-based max pain is the primary reference.
0DTE / Expiration day, Volume is high Volume MP takes the lead. On expiration day, new 0DTE positions drive most of the market maker hedging. OI MP still matters for non-0DTE expirations on the same date.
Rule of thumb: The further from expiration, the more OI MP matters. On expiration day, Volume MP becomes the primary signal. Always cross-reference with GEX — if GEX is positive near max pain, the price is more likely to stay pinned there.
GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Measures how much market makers need to hedge at each strike price.
Net GEX(K) = Call Gamma(K) × Call OI(K) × 100 − Put Gamma(K) × Put OI(K) × 100
Positive GEX (green bars): Market makers sell when price rises, buy when price falls → suppresses volatility, price tends to stay pinned.
Negative GEX (red bars): Market makers buy when price rises, sell when price falls → amplifies moves, more volatile.
GEX Flip = the strike where net GEX crosses from positive to negative. Above the flip, price is "sticky"; below it, moves accelerate.
Gamma values are from CBOE delayed data (~15 min delay). GEX uses current OI, so it shares the same daily-update limitation as OI Max Pain.
